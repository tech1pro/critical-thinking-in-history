A different kind of history class

Content knowledge matters. So does what students do with it.

These lessons use historical questions to develop habits that serve students well beyond the classroom: weighing sources, identifying assumptions, taking another point of view, and making an evidence-based case.

01

Start with a problem

Give students a meaningful decision, tension, or question to investigate.

02

Work with evidence

Use documents, claims, and perspectives as material for reasoning, not just recall.

03

Make thinking visible

Ask students to explain how they reached a conclusion and revise when needed.

Created by a classroom teacher
